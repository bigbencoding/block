Laser Pointer

Instructions

In this project, try to dodge the cat!

First, duplicate all the code in the "Move Around" project and put it in the laser. Replace the "forever" block with the "repeat until ()" block from the Control tab and add a "go to random position" block from the Motion tab on top of the "repeat until ()" block.

Next, click on the cat icon on the right of the screen. Now, drag a "when ðŸš© clicked" block onto the workspace. Under that, go to the Motion tab and place a "go to random position" block. Go to the Control tab and place a "repeat until ()". Inside the "repeat until ()" block, place a "point towards(mouse pointer)" block and change it to "point towards (laser)". Under the "point towards (laser)" block, place a "move 10 steps" block and change the value to 3. Finally, go to the Sensing tab and drag the "touching (mouse-pointer)" block into the "repeat until ()" block. Change the value to laser.

Finally, click on the laser icon on the right of the screen, and go to the Sensing tab. Drag the "touching (mouse-pointer)" block into the "repeat until ()" block. Change the value to cat.

You Finished!

Try pressing the green flag. See your hard work play before you eyes!

Code Explained

The "repeat until ()" block is a type of loop. This time, it takes a conditional. The loop goes on until the cat touches the laser and the other way around. When that happens, the program gets killed. Another way of saying this is it gets terminated. The "go to random position" block brings the cat and the laser somewhere random on the map, so there are more possibilites of playing the game. The cat is told to always point at the laser (except if the touched) and move forward at the same time. This results of the cat always moving toward the laser.
